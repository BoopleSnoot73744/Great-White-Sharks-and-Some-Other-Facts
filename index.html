Great White Sharks

Anatomy

Great white sharks have a very different anatomy compared to humans. But that is to be expected from the kings and queens of the sea.

One of the first most notible things about them are their big, sharp teeth. Their teeth are used to slice up the food that enters their mouth as they can't chew. They cut up the food and swallow those pieces whole.

Another notible feature of great white sharks and sharks in general are their pitch black eyes.If you have ever seen a great white shark eat,you probably have also seen their eyes kind of flicker. This is them actually moving their eyes behind their brain to protect them.

The final part of great white shark's anatomy I would like to talk about is their snout. Their snout is very important to the shark. It helps them sense the electricity created by fish. This allows them to hunt food easier and helps them navigate.

Behavior Towards Humans

Great white shark's behavior towards humans are kind of indifferent. They don't seem to like the taste of humans and just kind of don't about them.They will likely avoid you are just swim past. They might circle around you to see if you are food, but will promptly leave. However, they love headpats.They will settle down and just relax if you pat their heads. Lots of people might think that sharks are ferocious creatures that have a thirst for blood but they really aren't. Most of the times shark attacks are just misunderstandings and sharks mistaking humans as overgrown seals.

During the rare occurence of a shark attack, one should punch the tip of the snout or the eye. Those are the most sensitive regions of the head and will most likely make them release, if they dont realease by themselves. Shark related deaths are rare and you have a higher chance of getting killed by a cow of by bees then death by a shark attack.
